Data art

We are pleased to announce the Call for Submissions for DataArt2024. Data art is a form of artistic expression that leverages data to create visually captivating and conceptually rich artworks. The topic merges the worlds of data visualisation, digital art, and traditional art to convey complex information or concepts in visually compelling and engaging ways. In data-art, there are numerous avenues through which data can be utilised creatively; data can be the subject matter, directly mapped to visual forms, an underpinning fundamental component, medium, foundational data, or inspiration.

We solicit artworks for exhibition, with accompanying manuscript. The artworks must be suitable for wall display in the exhibition that will take place between 20-27 May 2024 in Pontio, Bangor University. A public opening and hands-on data-art workshop will take place on the 19th May. The event aims to bring together researchers, scholars, artists, hobbyists, and practitioners, to share their latest innovative ideas, and explore new avenues in data art.

Topics include, but not limited to:

  • Environmental data and sustainable themes
  • Data visualisations from data
  • Spatial data and geoinformation
  • Art, from Open Data sets, such as data.gov
  • Visual art pictures from biological data
  • Renderings of three-dimensional datasets
  • Social network visualisations
  • Data visualization as a form of artistic expression
  • Historical data
  • Re-rendering of visualisations
  • Collage or montage of different visualisations
  • Personal data
  • Artwork from sport and performance data
  • Text art from data
  • Pictures from data created from sounds, music, spoken words
  • Art from words, books, stories
  • Narrative and storytelling artwork

Requirments and general expectations

We invite submissions of original data-art works, along with accompanying manuscript, on various aspects related to data-art. We encourage new, novel, and interesting art pieces.
  1. Artworks must be original, and the author(s) must hold appropriate permissions and copyright,
  2. Artworks must be presentable for wall display, and works must be able to be shown as figures in a report. Artworks must be below A1 size. We envisage most art works to be A4 to A3 in size in print form.
  3. Artworks must represent data, or inspired-by data in some way

    4. We encourage, and permit, submitted works to be based on research. This could be from funded research and other projects. But submissions and the manuscript must be original, and different from any published works, this includes all the submitted material, including written text and images.

    The work must be created and authored by the author(s), who must hold copyright and relevant permissions to present data or pictures thereof. Submissions will be edited into an accompanying monograph, that will be published, and arranged by the organisers. Acknowledgements of funders, data sources, and other acknowledgements must be added to the submitted manuscript. References and relevant citations to related work, inspirations, techniques, or published works must be include, if suitable, up to a maximum of 5 references.
